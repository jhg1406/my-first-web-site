Baseball

It is a ball sport in which two teams of 10 players compete for victory or defeat by alternating between offense and defense for 9 innings each. The batter on the offensive side gets one point if he hits the ball thrown by the opposing pitcher with his bat, goes around 1st, 2nd, and 3rd base, which are specific points in the stadium, and returns home. If a batter or runner is out three times, offense and defense alternate and the game ends when the 9th inning is completed. Ultimately, the team with more points wins. If there is no game in the 9th inning, the Korean and Japanese leagues extend the game to the 12th inning, and if there is no game, it is decided as a draw (however, in the postseason, the game is played in the 15th inning), and in the United States, it is a win-loss game without a draw. It is a final battle until a decision is made.
