SERIE A

Serie A (Italian: Serie A ˈsɛːrje ˈa[*][1]), also known as Serie A TIM[2] after its sponsor TIM, is the highest level of football in the Italian football league system, with the winning team receiving the Small Shield. and receive the Italian Cup. This league was played in the current league format starting from the 1929-30 season. Our company, which organizes the league, was the secretariat of the upper league until 1943, and was then hosted by the national professional league secretariat until 2010, and is currently in charge of the national professional Serie A league secretariat from the 2010-11 season to the present. Serie A is one of the best leagues in the world and has a reputation as a league that focuses on tactics and stable defense.[3] IFFHS selected Serie A as the best league in 2020,[4] and the UEFA coefficient based on the performance of the Champions League and Europa League, the European competitions over the past five years, ranks one notch above Ligue 1, following La Liga, Bundesliga, and Premier League. He is ranked 4th. Previously, Serie A led the league by UEFA coefficient from 1986 to 1988 and again from 1990 to 1999.[5]
